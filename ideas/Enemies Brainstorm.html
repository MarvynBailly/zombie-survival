The bestiary today is 14 specialists strong — Cluster, Hivesac, Shrieker, Brood Mother, Necromancer, Charger, Reaper, Stalker, Bloater, Frost Walker, Mimic, Centipede, Hatchling, Twins — all wired around the walker as the baseline silhouette.

This brief widens the threat surface in a different direction: not just more variants of "dead person". There are humans who hate you, mutations that don't read as walkers at all, horde behaviors that change how groups feel, biome predators that own a single terrain, and the living wildlife the infection has corrupted.

Each card hooks into existing AI (aggro, pathfinding, spawn director). None duplicate the current bestiary. Pick which to spec.

THE RAIDERS

A faction of survivors who decided you're easier than walkers.

A new raider faction: armed humans organized into 4–8 person camps that spawn at fenced POIs (gas stations, lumber camps, the outer edge of cities). They patrol, set ambushes on roads, and actively hunt any player they've spotted within the last in‑game day.

Crucially, they fight zombies too — walk into a 3‑way engagement and the bullets are real. Their loot drops are tier‑locked weapons (the iron‑chest stuff, but on a living target). Killing a camp gives you the camp.

CAMP SIZE4–8 raiders
PATROL2‑hex radius from camp
MEMORY24 in‑game hours

LOOTiron‑tier weapons

LOOTERS

Won't fight. Will sprint past you with what you wanted.

Cowardly NPCs who spawn near chests at the same time you do — they grab loot and bolt for the world edge. Catch and kill to get their pack; let them escape and the chest's already empty. Adds urgency to looting without changing combat math.

CULTISTS

They worship the infected. They want to be them.

Robed humans who don't shoot — they charge with crude blades, ignore zombies entirely (zombies ignore them back, mostly), and deliberately inject themselves with infection on low HP, turning into a Charger‑class walker mid‑fight. Found around makeshift altar POIs in forest biomes.

THE BOUNTY

One named human. One scope. He has your last seed.

A single elite NPC who spawns once per run, at a random distance. He has a name, a rifle, and a line of fire on you any time you're in open terrain. Kills are visible as a red dot blinking on the compass. He breaks contact, repositions, and comes back. Adds a slow‑burn antagonist.

Water tiles aren't safe anymore.

Aquatic infected that live in water tiles — invisible until you enter, then surface and lunge. Fishing dock POIs become genuinely dangerous. Can also cross water to reach you, which a normal walker can't. Burns easy with the flamethrower if you can reach the shore.

PACK FLANKING

Some of them go around.

A behavior layer, not a new enemy. When a horde of 5+ walkers chases you, 30% of them route to flanks instead of the direct line — using the existing pathfinder with a small lateral offset. Suddenly "back away while shooting" doesn't work, because two of them are behind you.

Costs nothing to add; transforms how every existing encounter reads.

STAMPEDE

A horde at full sprint can break wood.

Walkers in a tight group of 8+ within 4 tiles of each other build a hidden momentum stat. Once it caps, they sprint together at 1.6× speed and a collective ram deals wall damage on contact — tier‑1 wood walls cave in 2 hits. Brick still holds. Real reason to upgrade your perimeter.

A single very evolved zombie. 3 AM only.

WHAT NEXT

The first‑order question for this brief is do you want humans in the game. Cluster A (Raiders, Looters, Cultists, Bounty) is the biggest direction; everything else is incremental. If yes, A·01 is the foundation and the rest stack.

Cluster B (Mutations) reads as the safe expansion — same content pipeline as the existing bestiary. Cluster C (Horde Behavior) is the cheapest and arguably the highest impact per line of code; recommend pairing C·01 + C·04 with whatever else you commit to.
