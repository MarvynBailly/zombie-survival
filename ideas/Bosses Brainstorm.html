The bestiary has one true boss — the Centipede — and one mini‑boss in the Brood Mother. Everything else in the roster is a specialist walker variant. There's no apex layer above the horde, and no fight you tell stories about.

This brief adds 19 boss directions plus 1 meta system, spread across five clusters: World Bosses (the immobile set‑pieces), Human Bosses (named antagonists who run factions), Hunts (mobile bosses who track you), Mechanic Bosses (encounters defined by an unusual rule), and Meta (how bosses fit into the run loop).

Each card hooks into existing systems — the bestiary draw pipeline, the spawn director, POI variants. Pick the ones that excite you and we'll spec.

THE COLOSSUS

A skyscraper of fused infected. It doesn't move much. It doesn't need to.

One per region, anchored to a unique landmark POI (the airport, the stadium). Six stories of meshed corpses, four primary weak points (eye clusters at 3 elevations, a glowing heart in the lower torso). Each weak point is a discrete phase: pop the eyes to expose the heart, kill the heart to drop the giant.

It generates a slow 20‑tile horde aura — regular walkers crawl out of its body every 10s. The encounter is "siege a building." Mythic loot table; killing it permanently changes the regional spawn rate by ‑15%.

THE NURSERY

A wall of pulsing sacs. Each one is a Brood Mother in larval form.

Immobile boss filling a 6×6 chamber inside an instanced dungeon. Six pulsing sacs around a central heart — each sac you ignore hatches a Brood Mother in 90s. Kill the heart first, but the heart's armored until 3+ sacs are dead. A timing puzzle.

Aquatic boss anchored to a large swamp / coastal lake. Fight from boat or pier — the boss circles, surfaces to lunge, dives. Three‑phase pattern that ends with a beach attempt where it briefly drags itself onto shore. Drops a unique speargun. Reuses Swimmer (Enemies B·02) art language.

THE WHITE STALKER

A Stalker that hunts the hunter.

Once you kill 10 regular Stalkers, the White Stalker appears as a permanent nemesis in your save. It tracks you across the map — you'll see its footprints on dust tiles, hear a distinct chitter when it's within 8 tiles. It only attacks when you're at <50% HP. Survive 3 encounters to "kill" it; drops a unique invisibility cloak (limited‑use stealth tool).

Controls every walker within 30 tiles like marionettes.

Mid‑sized humanoid boss who himself only deals minor damage — but every walker in a 30‑tile radius becomes directly controlled by him, moving in unnatural patterns (synchronized lunges, defensive rings around him, perfect flanking). THE MIRROR

Damage you deal, it deals back to you. Within seconds.

Reflective shell. Every bullet that hits it leaves the same damage as a delayed return projectile — a glowing red orb that floats toward your last position 4 seconds later. Survival mechanic: keep moving, dodge your own reflections, time your fire. Anti‑DPS — heavy weapons get punished. Crossbow and slow trigger discipline win.

Two layers of reality. Catch it in the right one.

Boss exists on a 2‑second cycle alternating between two visual states — solid and ghost. Bullets only land on solid frames; melee only lands on ghost frames (it can't dodge a swing it can't see). Track the cadence, alternate your toolkit. Spawns in the metro tunnels.

NAMED BOSSES

Every boss instance has a name. The world remembers them.

When a boss spawns for the first time in a seed, the game rolls a name + title + one trait from procedural tables ("Argos, the Watcher · regenerates"). Their name shows on the encounter banner, in the death screen, and in the Trophy Room (Homestead E·01). The trait is a real combat modifier — slight, but real.

Tiny system, huge identity payoff per run. The Brood Mother in your save isn't just "a Brood Mother" — it's Margery, the Mother of Worms.

ARENAS & PHASES

A boss isn't an HP bar — it's a room with rules.

Standardized boss arena system: every boss POI generates a walled circular arena (no escape during the fight) with arena features drawn from a pool: spawners, barrels, cover pillars, a fuel pool, climbable rooftops. Each boss specifies which features its arena uses.

Also defines phase transitions as a shared system: at 66% / 33% HP a boss can trigger an arena change (lights cut, barrels ignite, walls drop). One framework, every boss benefits.

SCALED VARIANTS

Same boss, three tiers. Loot scales with risk.

Every boss has 3 difficulty variants tied to region tier — Hatchling (50% HP, basic moveset), Adult (baseline), Apex (1.5× HP + extra mechanic). The player sees the tier as a visual cue (size, color). Adult drops the named weapon, Apex drops the named weapon with a mythic trait.

BOSS JOURNALS

Every kill writes itself into a permanent book.

After each boss kill, an entry is automatically written into a bestiary journal at the meta level: boss's rolled name, day killed, weapon used, time alive, attempts. Browse it from the menu screen. Slow accumulation; over many runs you're building a personal field guide. Pairs cleanly with the Cork Board prop.
