FOUNDATIONS & FLOORS

Build up. A real second story. A real watchtower.

Adds a Z‑axis to the build system. Place a floor tile on top of any 4‑wall room — it becomes the ceiling of room 1 and the deck of room 2. Stairs / ladders connect them. Walkers can't climb without a Climber spawning at the door.

The killer use: elevated firing platforms. From a 2nd‑story window your range and sightline both extend, and melee zombies just paw at the wall below. The texture of a base run changes from "thicker walls" to "higher ground."

TIER UP

Wood. Brick. Reinforced steel.

Walls now have three material tiers. Wood (existing): cheap, 200 HP, burns. Brick: 5× scrap, 600 HP, no burn. Reinforced steel: rebar + welded plate, 1500 HP, sparks bullets. Tap a wall + hold U to upgrade in place.

BLUEPRINTS

Save your base layout. Paste it next run.

Hold B to capture a 16×16 area as a blueprint. Stored in your meta profile. New runs: hit B at any empty tile to ghost‑project the layout, then build pieces as you collect materials. Ends the punishment of "I have to plan the same fortress from scratch every time."

PREFABS

A whole 4×4 shack. Drop‑placed. 60 seconds.

Workbench unlocks pre‑fab modules: guard shack (4‑wall + door), workshop addon, watchtower stub. Costs more total scrap than building piece‑by‑piece, but goes up in one button press. Great for the first night when minutes matter.

KITCHEN

Raw cans heal 10. A hot meal heals 60.

Build a cook station (range + counter, costs scrap + a found stovetop). Combine 2–3 raw ingredients (canned beans, jerky, herbs) into cooked meals with timed buffs: stew +stamina, chili +heat resist, coffee +sprint. The Garden feeds it.

Day phase gets a satisfying loop: forage, cook, buff, run.

GARDEN PLOTS

Plant seeds. Wait 3 days. Eat again.

Tilled plot tile (costs scrap + a hoe). Drop a seed, water it once per day from a bucket or rain‑barrel, harvest after 3 in‑game days. Crops: tomato (heal), chili (buff fuel for kitchen), poppy (medical, infection resist).

Renewable food source means a player can commit to a base long‑term without starving — a missing piece for late‑game loops.

GARAGE

A roofed bay for the truck you finally got running.

A 4×3 build piece — wide doorway, roof tile, slot for one vehicle (from the vehicles brief). Parked inside, the car refuels slowly from a generator and zombies can't damage it. Repair bench inside fixes bumper damage. Make the vehicle a real possession, not a one‑use husk.

A wired desk that talks to the rest of the world.

1. Call a Trader to your base (once/day). 2. Broadcast a survivor signal — slowly increases recruit spawn chance in nearby POIs. 3. Tune in on a Blood Moon for early warning ("RED SKY in 18 hrs").

WATCHTOWER

Six tiles tall. You can see them coming.

A dedicated build piece — small footprint, vertical. Climb the ladder to reach the deck, where your view radius doubles and you get a +20% accuracy halo for the Crossbow and Rifle. Place a watch survivor there from the squad system and they'll ping incoming hordes at 200 tiles.

MOAT & PITS

A trench full of spikes is just very good wall.

Dig terrain (new verb: shovel tool) to drop tiles by one level. Trenched tiles slow walkers to 40% and deal 30 dmg/sec. Spike them with sharpened rebar for +60 dmg on entry. Tanks ignore trenches — they just walk through, but the slow gives you time.

Pairs beautifully with Foundations: dig the moat, then build the wall on the inside lip.

NOISE BOX

A speaker on a stick. Bait them anywhere but here.

Place at any distance from base. Tap a hotkey to blare a 200‑tile aggro pull for 30 seconds; zombies route toward it instead of you. Runs on a battery (3 uses). Reverses the usual problem: instead of hiding from a horde, you're orchestrating one.

CAMERAS / CCTV

Four feeds. One screen. The map fills in itself.

Mount a camera (powered prop) anywhere within 80 tiles of base. Returns a small permanent reveal in the fog of war + a live feed on a monitor at base. Slot up to 4 monitors for a security wall. Late‑game vision tool — see a horde forming before it leaves the treeline.

SAFEHOUSE NETWORK

Three bases. A road between. Loot routes.

Allow up to three claimed bases at once — each marked on the map with a flag. Inventory and the weapon wall are per‑base: stash supplies wherever you find them. Fast‑travel between flags during the day only, costs 30s of in‑game time per hex.

Turns the 32k map from a single‑pin run into a route. A swamp outpost, a downtown clinic, a highland barn — all yours.

M
DECAY & UPKEEP

Walls weather. Rain rots wood. Nothing is forever.

Each wall has a hidden condition that drops 1–3% per day (faster under rain/blizzard). At 50% it visibly degrades; at 0% it's just rubble blocking a tile. Repair with scrap at a workbench in seconds, but you have to remember.

Gives the day phase a quiet routine: walk the perimeter, patch the soft spots. Bases that get neglected fall.

RAID NIGHT

Every 4th night, they come for you.

Standard nights spawn around the player. Raid Nights spawn a directional horde — 60+ walkers, 2 mini‑bosses — that pathfinds straight to your nearest claimed base. Announced at dusk. Designed for the perk Engineer + power grid + traps payoff.

THE RV

A base on wheels. Park where the run goes.

One unique vehicle — find as a wreck in the highland, repair with rare parts. Holds one bed + one workbench + 30 inventory slots. Park it in any biome and it counts as a claimed safehouse with weak walls (HP 400, flammable). Mobile but vulnerable. Pure trade‑off.

TROPHY ROOM

Mount the head of the Tank that almost got you.

Boss kills drop a trophy — a Brood Mother's egg sac, a Charger's horn, a Tank's plate of skull. Mount on a wall plinth at base. Hover to see day killed, weapon used, time alive. Pure narrative payoff, costs nothing to maintain.

The trophy room is the part of the run that survives death: meta‑saved between runs, stamped with the seed they came from.

CAMPFIRE BONDING

Sit. Wait. Listen to your squad.

Build a campfire inside the base. At dusk, hold E on it with squad members present — a 90‑second cutscene plays where they swap procedurally generated stories ("Before this, I was a substitute teacher. I taught fourth grade. My class was..."). Survivors who bond gain +15% accuracy the next day.

Tiny system, enormous emotional return. The squad you lose actually means something.

SQUAD QUARTERS

Bunks. Footlockers. A place to lose them from.

Bunk bed prop — each bed sleeps one squad member overnight, restoring their HP. Without a bunk they sleep on the floor (no recovery, –morale). Footlocker shows the loadout you've given them: medic gets the AR, engineer gets the wrench.
