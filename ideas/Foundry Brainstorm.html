The pitch is simple: you stop finding ammo and start making it — at industrial scale. The Foundry is a multi‑stage production network you bolt onto the homestead. Pull crude oil from a wildcat well, dig iron and copper from veins, refine the slurries into powder, plastics, brass strip, lead cores, primer compound, and feed them down a belt to an assembly line that prints finished cartridges by the crate.

The point isn't a recipe — it's a factory. Belts. Pipes. Hoppers. Couriers. A foreman's console. The first hour is hauling jerricans by hand. By night fifty you're running an automated city block that wakes up while you sleep.

It is also a beacon. Every smokestack pulls aggro from a mile out, sour crude leaks toxic gas, and there's a special zombie that clogs your pipes for fun. Set‑up is a project. Defending the project is a second project. Eat it, gunslinger.

WILDCAT OIL FIELD

Step one. Find the seep. Sink the well. Pray for sweet.

Oil isn't on the map — you scout for it. A handful of oil seeps spawn per world (dark stain on a tile, faint smell radius). You sink a pumpjack on top of it (rare blueprint, found at a fueling‑station POI). It pulls crude into a buried tank at 1 barrel/min, automated.

Two crude qualities, rolled per well: sweet (clean, high powder yield) or sour (foul, 1.5× yield but vents a toxic gas cloud 8 tiles wide — kills crops, sickens survivors, scrubs need a flare stack). The decision to claim a sour field is half the meta game.

Wells deplete. A rich one dries in ~20 days; you'll go nomadic, or run a pipeline (see E·04).

ORE DRILL

Plant it on a vein. It eats stone. You collect ingots.

Three vein types spawn in the highlands and quarry POI: iron (everywhere), copper (cliffs), lead (caves; toxic, sickens unprotected workers). Hand‑mine with a pickaxe for slow yield, or plant a powered drill for 1 chunk/30s automated.

Veins are finite. Surveyed remaining tonnage shows on the drill — you'll pull up stakes and move it.

SALT & SULFUR

The boring half of black powder. Hand‑dug from caves.

Saltpeter (cave crystals) and sulfur (yellow vents near hot springs) round out the powder recipe. No automation — by design. You hand‑mine these on scavenger runs and haul them home in stacks. Keeps the early loop physical.

A late‑game Saltpeter Vat can synthesize it from bat guano + lye — funny, slow, but a renewable backstop.

BRASS SCAVENGERS

Send a survivor crew out to pick up your spent casings.

Every shot you fire leaves a casing on the ground. Assign a survivor (from the homestead brief) to a brass crew; they patrol within 60 tiles and return a bag of casings to a hopper at base each day.

Brass cases reduce assembly cost by 60% — recycled rounds are your bread and butter. Without a crew, only what you personally pick up survives.

REFINERY TOWER

One crude in. Four named fluids out.

The cracking tower is the spine of the Foundry. It takes crude and a constant trickle of coal, runs hot, and outputs four products on four separate pipes:

SMOKELESS BASE · feed it to the powder mill.
CASING PLASTIC · the polymer for shotgun hulls and shock‑round bodies.
LUBRICANT · keeps every machine in the Foundry above 80% condition. Run out and your factory jams.
FUEL OIL · same fuel that runs vehicles, generators, chainsaws, and flamers.

You set the blend dial per tower — bias more powder vs more fuel. The numbers update live on the foreman console. Towers must be flared (B·05) or they back up and shut down.

BLAST FURNACE

Raw ore in the door. Ingots out the chute.

Smelts iron / copper / lead chunks into stackable ingots. Burns coke (from B·05) at 2/ingot. Three slots: fuel, ore, output. Cycle 12s.

Lead smelting vents toxic fumes inside a 4‑tile radius — keep it on an outer wall or build a flue.

CENTRIFUGE

Spin slurry. Heavy on one side, light on the other.

Takes powdered minerals + water and separates them by density. The key use: saltpeter slurry → pure saltpeter + lye byproduct; sulfur muck → pure sulfur + acid byproduct. Also used to enrich primer compound (D·02 ammo types).

The byproducts aren't waste — lye is soap (survivor morale), acid pairs with the Acid Round recipe.

HOOKSwaterbyproductsacid rounds
B·04
M
POWDER MILL

Where smokeless meets saltpeter. Try not to spark anything.

Combines smokeless base + saltpeter + sulfur + charcoal on a rotating drum into finished propellant. Three recipes — fast (pistol/SMG), medium (rifle), slow (shotgun, AP). The recipe you load determines what rounds the line downstream can make.

The mill is volatile. A fire within 3 tiles, or a stray bullet in the building, has a chance to trigger a chain detonation. Build it underground or in its own outbuilding.

ASSEMBLY LINE

A belt, four stations, a crate at the end.

The terminal stage. A short conveyor with four station slots — drop in casing, then primer, then powder charge, then projectile. Each station is a separate building piece you've placed; the line connects them.

The same line can produce any caliber — you load a recipe card at the foreman console (D·04). Switching cards stalls the line for 20s while the arms reconfigure. Finished rounds drop into a crate at the end of the belt; full crates can be hauled to your weapon wall, your turret feeds, or a trade caravan.

Throughput is ~4 rounds/sec at full feed. The bottleneck is always upstream — whichever precursor runs out first stops the whole line. Watching the foreman view is a tiny puzzle game in itself.

CASING PRESS

Hydraulic stamps brass strip into deep‑drawn cups.

Brass ingot + lubricant → casings in the caliber you've loaded. Slow (5s/case); the heart of throughput. Recycled brass (from A·04 scavengers) skips the smelting step and runs 2× faster.

Each press dies for one caliber family — swap the die plate to produce 9mm vs. 12‑gauge. Crafted as a workbench upgrade.

PRIMER BENCH

Tiny disks. Big bang if you sneeze on them.

Mixes lead styphnate + acid byproduct + powdered glass into primer compound, presses it into copper cups. Cycle 2s/primer, but the bench needs a survivor operator — too dangerous to fully automate, and a higher operator skill = lower defect rate.

A jammed primer is the source of weapon misfires (E·03 quality).

SPECIALTY LINES

Branch the line to make boutique rounds in parallel.

A second (and third, and fourth…) line shares your precursor supply but takes a rare additive at one station. Run them in parallel and your factory makes a mix of round types simultaneously.

INCENDIARY · +1 white phosphorus / round.
ARMOR‑PIERCING · steel‑penetrator projectile (extra blast furnace pass).
ACID · centrifuge acid byproduct charge.
CAPACITOR CELL · the Railgun's food. End‑game; needs a charged capacitor at station 4 plus a copper coil.

BELTS · PIPES · INSERTERS

Three modular parts. Plumb your whole factory with them.

The connective layer. You place these like building pieces and they snap onto adjacent machines automatically.

BELTS · move solid items in a straight line at 1 tile/sec. Underground belt segments tunnel for 4 tiles. T‑junctions split by priority or by item type (a filter).
PIPES · move fluids — crude, lube, fuel, plastic. Direction is automatic (machine pressure differential). One‑way valves let you isolate sections for repair.
INSERTERS · little arms that lift one item off a belt and feed it into a machine, or vice versa. Stack them at junctions to handle multiple item types.

Belts and pipes have an HP value — a zombie can claw through one if it's exposed. Run them underground (more cost, safe), or build a wall over them, or run them outside the perimeter and accept the risk. This is the puzzle: every factory layout is a balance of throughput, cost, and how exposed your tubing is.

SILOS & TANKS

Smooth out the spikes. A factory needs buffer.

Silos hold solids (ore, brass, powder, primer); tanks hold fluids. Stackable up to 3‑high for vertical storage. Read‑outs on the side show fill % at a glance — same colors that show up on the foreman console.

Critical for the night cycle: build deep enough buffer and your factory keeps running while you're scavenging.

COURIER CARTS

A mine cart on rails between two hoppers.

For long hauls where a belt is impractical (remote oil well to base, or two distant ore drills). Lay rail, plant two hopper stations, the cart shuttles automatically. Carries 20 stacks per trip. Slow but cheap.

Alt: assign a survivor courier who walks the same route on foot with a backpack — slower, smarter, can defend itself.

FOREMAN CONSOLE

One panel. The whole factory at a glance.

Build the console and a top‑down x‑ray of your Foundry appears on its screen. Live throughput per belt, fill % per silo, condition per machine, alarms for bottlenecks. Click a machine on the panel to fast‑travel the camera.

Plus a production planner: tell it "I want 200 rounds of 12ga incendiary," and it highlights what's missing — out of phosphorus, lube tank at 11%, etc. Becomes the home base of the engineering player.

THE BIG SMOKE

Every chimney is a beacon. Every shift gathers a crowd.

Each running machine emits a stench radius (worse for refineries and furnaces, light for benches). Stench radii stack on the tile map and produce a slow aggro pull — wanderer zombies will drift toward your base over hours of game time, not just react to gunfire.

Counters: build flare stacks (E·04 byproduct), pipe machines underground, install filtration scrubbers (cost: spare lubricant + a HEPA filter, rare). Pick your poison: scale down, or scale up and defend.

THE GREMLIN

A new zombie. Small, fast, eats pipes.

Once your Foundry pulls aggro from far enough, Gremlins show up at night. Sewer‑born and small enough to fit through ventilation ducts. They don't attack you — they crawl onto your belts and pipes and clog them. A clogged segment stalls everything downstream.

You won't notice from inside your bedroom — only the foreman console alarm. Late‑game players will build Gremlin baffles (1‑tile screen pieces) at every duct, and patrol routes at dawn to clear nests. A real cost to a real reward.

E·03
M
MATCH GRADE

Five quality tiers. Tolerances earn the upper ones.

Every round leaves the line with a quality roll: Salvage / Standard / Match / Boutique / Experimental. The roll is weighted by inputs:

1) machine condition (well‑lubricated, recently maintained), 2) primer operator skill, 3) recipe purity (sweet crude beats sour for headshot crit, sour crude beats sweet for damage). Match‑grade rounds give +25% headshot crit; Boutique unlocks the trait pool from Named Weapons (ARM‑001 E·01) on the cartridge — yes, a named bullet.

HOOKSconditionnamed weaponssurvivor skills
E·04
XL
THE PIPELINE

A 200‑tile umbilical from a far well to your tanks.

When your local oil wells dry up (or you find a rich sour one across the map), you commission a pipeline: a long pipe segment that hauls crude across the world. Construction is the real game — you build it tile by tile, defending the work site at dusk.

Pipelines become permanent defensive concerns. Every tile is HP. A pipeline rupture costs barrels of crude and lights a fire. Place pumping stations every 40 tiles (they boost flow + double as fast‑travel waypoints + bunkers). Survivors can be stationed at stations as guards.

An entire late‑game faction war can be fought over whose pipeline runs through the highway POI.

WHAT NEXT

This brief is intentionally too ambitious. Read it as a maximum shape — the full Foundry would be a months‑long content track on its own. The interesting question is which slice ships in the first cut.

Three honest first‑cut bundles, in order of effort:

  1. MICRO · A·02 ore drill + B·02 furnace + C·02 casing press + the ARM‑001 ammo press. No belts, no oil. Hand‑hauled. Doable in a week of work.
  2. MIDI · adds A·01 oil + B·01 refinery + B·04 powder mill + C·01 assembly line + D·01 belts. The factory becomes real but stays small.
  3. MAXI · adds D·02–04 logistics, all of Cluster E, the named‑bullet bridge, the pipeline. Endgame‑worthy.

Tell me which cards survive a strike‑through pass and which two or three you want me to spec or prototype first — the foreman console UI and an automated assembly line are both fun visual mockups.
