The world right now: 32k×32k procgen, deterministic per‑chunk seeds, terrain classified into grass / forest / water / mountain. POIs go from hut at low tiers up through city (36×36 tiles), plus terrain‑flavored variants (fishing dock, lumber camp, mining outpost, farm). Buildings are procedurally laid out inside each POI footprint.

That's a great floor — but the city POI is still "a denser hut", and the world reads as a noise field with buildings scattered on top. This brief pushes in five directions: making cities feel like cities, adding landmark POIs the player will actually remember, fixing exploration legibility, opening up hidden spaces, and texturing the empty miles between things.

None of these duplicate the prior briefs. Each card respects the existing chunk/POI/zone system. Tell me which to push.

DISTRICTS

Downtown. Industrial. Suburbs. Harbor. Each looks different.

City POIs are currently a uniform grid of buildings. Split each city's footprint into 3–5 districts, each with its own theme: Downtown (tall narrow buildings, dense), Industrial (warehouse + factory geometry, fuel barrels, fenced yards), Residential (rows of houses with backyards), Harbor (only on coastal cities, piers + crates), Commercial (storefronts + mall).

The split is a Voronoi cut on the POI footprint, seeded from the city's existing seed — fully deterministic, fully cached. Each district uses a different building emitter from the existing palette.

ROAD GRID

A real network. POIs hang off it.

Right now POIs sit in scattered zones. Add a connecting road layer: stamp paved tiles between every city/town pair within 6 zones, with smaller gravel spurs to outlying POIs. Roads bias spawns (cars wreck on them, raider patrols use them). Players have something to follow.

HIGH RISES

A 3‑floor building you actually enter.

Within downtown districts, 10–20% of buildings get vertical interiors. Enter at street level, stair tile takes you to a separately‑generated floor 2, then floor 3. Top floors hold higher‑tier chests but the climb is one‑way (zombies follow). Hooks into the Foundations & Floors home‑base feature for shared rendering.

STREET PROPS

Mailboxes, benches, hydrants, bus stops, dumpsters.

City tiles are mostly empty between buildings. Add a procedural street decoration pass: per‑tile lookup that drops one of ~12 small props on sidewalk tiles. Most are decor; a few (mailbox, dumpster) hold scrap. Costs nothing at runtime — just makes "the empty block between two buildings" read.

Stamp the map. Color‑code it. Remember.

Right‑click anywhere on the map menu to drop a colored pin with a short label ("car works", "good chest", "dog cage"). Up to 32 pins per run, 8 per safehouse, meta‑saved. Tiny system, huge perceived control over an enormous map. Pins show on compass when within 200 tiles.

WHAT NEXT

The biggest single lever in this brief is A·01 Districts — it makes the city POI actually feel different from the town POI, which it currently doesn't. Pair it with A·02 Road Grid and E·02 Wreck Rivers and the whole worldmap reads as a connected place instead of scattered points.

The Landmarks (Cluster B) are content‑heavy but the highest narrative payoff per POI. The Exploration cards (C) are mostly small UI work — quick wins.
